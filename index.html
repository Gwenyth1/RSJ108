How To Write an Article ...

Assembly Time: 3-5 hours
Effectiveness Rating: 95% Effective

Are you wondering how to write a professional looking article? It's harder than it seems. Follow the steps and you'll be able to write an accurite and well structured article!

  • Things You'll Need

  • An Interviewee
  • Reporter Notepad
  • Voice Recorder
  • Computer
  • Camera
  • Peer or Mentor

  1. Choose Your Topic

    You can't write an article without a topic! Pick something you're interested in and narrow down the scope of your topic.

  2. Schedule Your Interviews

    TNow that you've chosen your topic, research and choose potential interviewees for your project. Reach out and schedule an interview in advance. Depending on your article, you may need multiple interviews. As a rule of thumb, shoot for at least three sources in your article (at least two interviews, and one peer reviewed journal or digital source).

  3. Conduct Your Interview

    Now that you've chosen your sources, conduct your interview! Remember to go in with about 5 questions and to ask followup questions. Ask if you can record your interview, and ALWAYS take detailed notes. Before you leave, take a few photos of your interviewee (if they give permission) and your environment to add into your story.

    'Voice Recorder' Keila Trejo on flickr: https://www.flickr.com/photos/iadmedia/26386243371/.

  4. Transcribe Your Interview

    Once you finish your interview, listen to your recording and transcribe any notable quotes or useful information. Remember to double check your quotes and any numbers before writing!

  5. Organize Information In Order From Most to Least Important

    Journalistic articles are formatted differently than academic writing! Make sure to write an interesting lead to catch your reader's attention, including the who, what, where, and why. Then, organize all of your information from most important to least important. Remember that you don't need a conclusion paragraph. Once you've run out of information, just stop writing!

  • Choose Your Best Photos

    Now that you have all of your important information, go through your photos and find the ones that fit the best with what you're trying to say in your article. Remember to cite your source and describe what is happening in the image.

  • Write Your Article!

    At this point in the process, you should have everything you need to begin writing. While your writing, try to take a write now/edit later mindset. This allows you to get all of your thoughts into writing, without losing your train of thought.

  • Edit (X3)

    When writing an article, it's important that everything is to the point, easy to read, and accurate. Make sure to cut out any unessessary words and sentances, making your article as streamlined as possible. Double and tripplecheck your quotes, numbers, and statistics for any mistakes.

  • Review With A Trusted Peer or Mentor

    Once you've finished writing, hand off your article to a trusted peer or mentor to read over. Having someone else read over your writing will help you edit for readability. Ask them to point out any moments of where they're not sure what you're refering to, or where they're confused so you can go back and edit once again.

  • Check For Accuracy

    Durring your final edit, check your facts, figures, and quotes for accuracy, and remember to rewrite for clarity. Please remember to check any captions (for images) and social media links.

  • Finish

    Now that you've rewriten and checked for accuracy, make any last minute changes and set your project aside. Finally, take a deep breath and congradulate yourself on a job well done!
